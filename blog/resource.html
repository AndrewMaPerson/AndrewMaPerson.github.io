I have a great time in school, reading and talking my way into middle and high school debate. Currently I’m Speech & Debate captain and my primary event is Extemp. In extemporaneous speaking, we are given 3 questions about current events, and we have 30 minutes to prepare a memorized, 7 minute long speech giving a nuanced answer backed by complex analysis, citing a good 6-10 sources along the way. It’s hard- I’m getting better every week. I also realized this was similar to Congressional debate, except with time constraints. Then I thought: Why not do Congressional debate in real life, and make an impact? I acted on that thought, and my mom generously drove me down to Olympia on my day off from school. So yes, I have become a “fast and smooth talker”, but I’m trying to use it for good. I told my crewmates (rowing) about what I did, and they were very surprised and wanted to get involved. They simply hadn’t known. Here is the website for the National Speech and Debate Association. You can learn about the different events that high schoolers do! (There's like 2 dozen.)
